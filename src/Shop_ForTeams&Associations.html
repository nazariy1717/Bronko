  • For Teams & Associations

Successful teams and associations have a program for player development which includes resources to help their players improve in numerous ways. Good coaching, access to quality trainers and therapists, off-ice training, video review, skating instructors, goalie coaches etc all contribute to the development package they offer their players.

When a new tool that can benefit their players becomes available they look at how they can incorporate it into their program to gain an advantage.

Teams typically put the Belt on before hitting the ice and wear the Belt for the first 15-20 minutes of practice.

The Bronko Belt is being used by a growing number of players and teams in Canada and the US because it’s a simple and effective tool that can be used to achieve a variety of significant benefits. It can be added to your current development program quickly and affordably.

Investing in the Belt as a team or association puts a valuable tool into the hands of your players and coaches at the lowest cost and sends a strong signal to current and prospective players, coaches and parents that player development is a high priority. It shows a willingness to embrace innovative and effective tools that can improve the player development package your organization offers.

Players can wear the Belt in a wide range of drills so coaches have the flexibility to plan effective practices.

Most importantly, it helps provide a range of real benefits to your players that can help them achieve more success on the ice. Enjoying more success on the ice and having a track record for developing players and helping them move on to the next level are two of the most important ways in which an organization can be measured. Both make it easier to recruit high caliber players and coaches.

Bronko Hockey works with interested teams and associations to create custom training solutions based on age, level, location and budget. Contact us to discuss how your team or association can incorporate the Bronko Belt into your player development program in a way that fits within your budget.

When instructed to do so by the coach, players remove the Belts during a water break and continue practice.
